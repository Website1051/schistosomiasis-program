Symptoms of Schistosomiasis

Introduction

Schistosomiasis symptoms vary based on the stage of infection. Some people experience mild or no symptoms, while others develop severe complications if left untreated.

Early Symptoms (Acute Phase)

Shortly after infection, individuals may experience the following symptoms:

  • Skin rash or itching at the infection site.
  • Fever, chills, and muscle aches.
  • Swollen lymph nodes.
  • Fatigue and headache.

Advanced Symptoms (Chronic Phase)

Over time, if untreated, the disease can cause significant organ damage:

  • Abdominal pain and diarrhea.
  • Blood in urine (urinary schistosomiasis) or stool (intestinal schistosomiasis).
  • Enlarged liver and spleen.
  • Difficulty urinating and increased risk of bladder cancer.

Severe Complications

In severe cases, schistosomiasis can lead to:

  • Severe liver damage and fibrosis.
  • Pulmonary hypertension (affecting the lungs).
  • Neurological complications if parasites reach the brain or spinal cord.
  • Increased risk of secondary infections due to a weakened immune system.

When to Seek Medical Help

If you experience persistent symptoms such as blood in urine or stool, difficulty breathing, or severe abdominal pain, consult a healthcare provider immediately for diagnosis and treatment.
